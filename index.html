About

My name is Sean Parrott-Wolfe. My path began with an interest in typography and letterpress printing, introduced to me by my father, a printer. That was around the time I began learning to program computers. Fascinated by the underlying structure and mechanics, I went on to study mathematics, particularly focused on cryptography and physics. I continue to learn by breaking things in order to reconstruct them; a cycle of destruction and creation. It is how I better understand how things work.

Passions

What I love to do.

I have a general interest in the transmission of information: how can it be secured, how it can be packaged or presented, and how science, mathematics, and technology can be used to solve these problems in new ways.
